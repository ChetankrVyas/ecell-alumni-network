IIT BHU Alumni Angel Network

IIT BHU Alumni Angel Network is being established to create an easy medium for the students and alumni entrepreneurs of IIT BHU to access funding opportunities for their startups and raise funds from successful and distinguished alums of IIT BHU. The sole purpose of this body is to help and support the startups coming from IIT BHU in the process of fund-raising by creating an easy-to-access platform for a all.

Structure of IIT BHU Alumni Angel Network:

Process:

Committee Zero:

Roles:

  • Ensuring a proper flow of information between all the other committees of this Angel Network.
  • Reaching out to fellow alumni and student entrepreneurs and publicizing this angel network.
  • Organizing the pitch day for the alumni and students startups who require funding.
  • Finding and reaching out to the best-fit alumni for the respective committees.

Roles:

  • Giving out some valuable insights on what amount is best suited for the investment in a particular startup based on the judging of the business plan and pitch deck.
  • Submitting a report on whether the startup is worth investing in or not.
  • Guiding and mentoring startups.

Due Diligence Committee:

Roles:

  • Checking whether the startup is fit for investment or not.
  • Checking whether the paperwork - Startup Registration, GST Filing, Privacy Policy, Bank Account, SHA, Terms and Conditions, etc. - are properly structured or not.

Group of Investors:

Structure:

  • This committee is the most important committee of all the above and below committees.
  • The ones who commit a funding amount for the funding of alumni or student startups, they will be considered to be the members of this committee.

Post-Investment Check Committee:

Roles:

  • This will committee will ensure that the startup is sharing all the relevant reports in a particular interval of time with the existing investors.

Structure:

  • This committee consists of two sub-committees: (i) General (ii) Startup Specific
  • (i) General: A generic committee responsible for the post-investment check for all startups in general.
  • (ii) Startup Specific: A committee formed by the lead investor of a startup working towards bringing out the most of the startup.

Partnerships Committee:

Roles:

  • Working towards bringing in services of companies such as AWS, Azure, ZOHO, etc. which are commonly required by the startups at discounted rates.

General Committee:

Roles:

  • Working towards helping the startups raise their next investment round by finding the best suitable investors for the respective startups.
